Project Plan

Overview

 

Topic 

What we propose to achieve with our project is the design and production of a device that can determine your current mental health and mood and assist you in improving on it. There is currently no known device that can replace human contact and the effect that has on us, this is especially important given the current global pandemic where many, if not all, of us are restricted to our homes and as a result have very limited human interaction.

 

Motivation 

In March of 2020, the World Health Organisation (WHO) declared the COVID-19 virus to be a global pandemic (Cucinotta and Vanelli, 2020).  Lockdowns became common practice for countries around the world, leading to global job-loss, nation-wide uncertainty and diminished levels of face-to-face interaction.  Due to this, the COVID-19 pandemic presented itself as a significant hazard to mental health globally (Xiong et al., 2020).  Through research studies conducted in 2021, it was found that the global prevalence of serious, clinical-level mental health issues had increased significantly in the populations of China, Spain, Italy, Iran, USA, Turkey, Nepal and Denmark; anxiety (6.33%-50.9%), depression (14.6%-48.3%), post-traumatic stress disorder (7%-53.8%), psychological distress (34.43%-38%) and stress (8.1%-81.9%) (Xiong et al., 2020).  The demand for mental-health support services increased significantly in the USA, 30% of mental-health support workers reported an increase in patients overall (Caron, 2021). However, there is a discrepancy in the demand for mental-health support and the amount of available mental-health support workers, leading to patients being denied diagnosis, treatment and assistance.   

 

Although no device known can currently replace human contact and connection. There needs to be a device that can check on your emotional and physical wellbeing and help improve it. This can be done with the EVA AI wellness assistant. The EVA will monitor your health through various sensors and a camera feed. The device will detect your mood and suggest the most appropriate action to take for any given situation. If you have been working too long and require a break, the EVA will prompt you and encourage you to relax. This is a much-needed device for such a time as this.

 

The development of this device is also benefited by the current advances in AI technology. As seen worldwide, the demand for AI to replace and enhance aspects of life is higher than ever and this new demand has made it easier to find and utilise AI tech that has already been developed in the process of building our device.

 

Landscape 

As stated previously there are currently no similar devices readily available on the market that we are aware of. To the best of our knowledge the closest devices available are Smart watches; such as the apple and Samsung watches and Fitbits. These make use of heartrate monitoring tech among other applications to measure the user’s physical wellbeing but aren't able to measure or evaluate an individual’s mental health as our product EVA would be able too. 

 

Detailed Description

Aims 

Our aim with the EVA AI wellness assistant is to design and produce a device that makes use of cameras placed around the house to determine the users’ mood and evaluate their current mental health status. We are aiming to ease the strain on the mental health system on a global scale. A device such as this within the users' home that is able to scan their face and track their movement and activity levels during their day and then gives prompts and advice to said user on how they can stop negative habits forming is crucial in preventing mental health illness.

 

Whilst EVA would not be able to diagnose and prescribe treatment to individuals, it would be able to detect patterns and the formation of habits that often lead to the development of mental health issues and illness. For example, if the user was to be working from home, just as many of us need to be, the EVA assistant would track any movements and eating habits and give prompts that the user could use to improve on and stop those negative habits before they form into poor mental health.

 

The EVA assistant will collect all the data it gains from the face scans, movement and mood tracking it does and will store all this data on secure networks that can only be accessed by licensed medical practitioners. This information can then be used to assist with diagnosis and treatment plans given to patients. This in turn, alleviates the pressure on medical professionals as they won't need to spend as much time with one patient, thus tending to more patients on any given day.

 

EVA can also store advice and treatment plans from those medical professionals so that it can track the user at home and ensure these advices and medication (if prescribed) are being used as directed. Reducing the need for follow up appointments but still ensuring the user is making improvements.

Tools and Technologies 

The programming language the EVA will utilize is Python, this is because of its functionality and ease of use with object-orientated programming. The Raspberry Pi hardware will have Python-based software which will be implemented. This will allow the EVA to run every time the Raspberry Pi is turned on.

 

The EVA wellness assistant runs on the Raspberry Pi 4 with the following specifications:  

·                Broadcom BCM2711, Quad core Cortex-A72 (ARM v8) 64-bit SoC @ 1.5GHz 

·                 2GB, 4GB or 8GB LPDDR4-3200 SDRAM (depending on model)  

·                2.4 GHz and 5.0 GHz IEEE 802.11ac wireless, Bluetooth 5.0, BLE  

·                Gigabit Ethernet  

·                2 USB 3.0 ports; 2 USB 2.0 ports.  

·                Raspberry Pi standard 40 pin GPIO header (fully backward compatible with previous boards)  

·                2 × micro-HDMI ports (up to 4kp60 supported)  

·                2-lane MIPI DSI display port  

·                2-lane MIPI CSI camera port  

·                4-pole stereo audio and composite video port  

·                H.265 (4kp60 decode), H264 (1080p60 decode, 1080p30 encode)  

·                OpenGL ES 3.1, Vulkan 1.0  

·                Micro-SD card slot for loading operating system and data storage  

·                5V DC via USB-C connector (minimum 3A*)  

·                5V DC via GPIO header (minimum 3A*)  

·                Power over Ethernet (PoE) enabled (requires separate PoE HAT)  

·                Operating temperature: 0 – 50 degrees C ambient  

 

Passive infrared (PIR) motion Sensors will be added to the EVA to detect how much movement the body is making while working, studying, or relaxing. This can help detect anxiety/stress and general wellbeing.   

 

A Webcam will be attached to the Raspberry Pi interface as well, this will be ideally be situated above the user's face or in a place where the face can be seen frequently. The EVA wellness assistant will be placed above the user's workspace where it can analyse and view the user as effectively as possible. The webcam will help detect; sleep deprivation, dehydration, mental illness, frequent breaks from work.

 

DHT22 sensors will be utilized to monitor the temperature of your living/working environment, this will help the EVA detect if your environment needs to be warmer or colder, depending on your mood and current needs. For example, if you are highly energetic in the present moment perhaps a slightly cooler atmosphere would help you stay focused. If you are feeling slightly fatigued a warmer temperature would be healthier for you in terms of easing your body into a rested state.

 

The technology mentioned above would require the following software; Python, OpenCV, Tensor Flow, Keras as well as connection to WIFI to function as intended. The hardware is the Raspberry Pi 4, with the camera module, DH22 heat sensors, and PIR Infrared motion sensors. 

  

Software

Python: This is a programming language that can cover a large range of purposes, from small to large-scale projects. It has an object-orientated focus that keeps the coding process simple and easy to comprehend.

 

OpenCV: This is a tool for image recognition and processing. It can do facial detection, tracking, and other vision-related tasks with the use of Artificial Intelligence.

 

Tensor Flow: Tensor flow is utilized particularly in the training of deep neural networks.

 

Keras: Is an open-source Python interface for neural networks.

 

Hardware

Raspberry Pi: A computer the size of a credit card developed in the United Kingdom.

 

Camera Module: The Raspberry Pi v2 Camera Module, has an 8-megapixel sensor and can take still photos as well as video recordings.

 

DH22 heat sensors: This is a heat and humidity sensor that uses a thermistor to measure the qualities of the surrounding air with a digital output.

 

PIR Infrared motion sensors: Motion sensors often used to detect human movement, very small and consume little power. Detecting large warm moving objects accurately and efficiently.
